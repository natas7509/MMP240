Food & Drink

Brixton Meadows features more delicious food than you could eat in a single weekend, with over 100 vendors serving everything from wood-fired pizza and hand-crafted burgers to authentic street tacos and fresh-from-the-wok pad thai. With plenty of options for every taste and budget, and ample menu items for vegetarian, vegan and gluten-free guests, we promise you’ll never go hungry.

Pop -Ups

Once again the 2018 Brixton Meadows festival will host a “pop-up” stand adjacent to the Meadows Stage to welcome breweries and distilleries selling products made with local ingredients. Every year New Yorkers can meet a different craft beverage producer, sample their products, and purchase a bottle or two to bring home. This year, the stand will come from the local restaurant group "55-Bar". In addition, the stand will be selling non-acloholic beer from regionally produced whole grains and flours. If beer is not your thing? check below for their superb cocktail menu.

Rich History

Considered by many as the first modern music festival that we would recognise today, the Brixton Meadows festival in queens, New York, was first held in August 1982. Plans were in place to transform a dairy farm into a two-day music event, and more than 100,000 tickets were sold. However, demand from the public was far greater than festival organisers had anticipated. As campers began to set up their tents and the artists started their sets on stage, the organisers were shocked to find that nearly half a million people descended upon the 700-acre farmland to watch the likes of The Clash, Duran Duran and The Cure.
